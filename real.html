Real Madrid

Real Madrid (along with Barcelona and Athletic) participated in all national championships. He was first 35 times, second 25, third 10 times, that is, in total the club was a winner in 70 seasons. Another 8 times he stopped one step away from the podium, finished fifth three times, 4 times sixth, 2 seventh, 1 eighth, 2 ninth, 1 tenth, 1 eleventh (in 1948).

The stadium was named "Santiago Bernabeu" in honor of the president of Real Madrid from 1943 to 1975, who initiated the construction of the new stadium. Construction lasted three years. The opening match was a friendly match with the Portuguese club Belenenses, the meeting took place on December 14, 1947 and ended in a victory for the hosts with a score of 3-1.

The Bernabeu hosted the 1964 European Championship final and the 2010 Champions League final. On November 14, 2007, the Bernabeu was awarded the status of an elite UEFA football stadium.

  • Real Madrid is one of only three clubs to have never been relegated from the Spanish top flight, the other two being Barcelona and Athletic Bilbao.
  • It is one of the most titled clubs in Spanish football.
  • In 2019, recognized as the most valuable football club, with a total value of $4.2 billion.
  • The club has made numerous expensive transfers, including the acquisition of Cristiano Ronaldo and Gareth Bale for record sums.
  • The matches between Real Madrid and Barcelona, ​​known as El Clasico, are considered to be some of the hottest and most watched matches in the world.
  • In the early 2000s, Real Madrid became known as the "Team Galactica" thanks to the acquisition of international stars such as Zinedine Zidane and Luis Figo.

The 2023/24 season will be Real Madrid's 120th season in its history and 93rd in La Liga, the top division of the Spanish football league system. During the summer off-season, several leading players such as Karim Benzema and Marco Asensio left the team. However, the management immediately began to sign new players to replace them: midfielder Jude Bellingham was bought from Borussia Dortmund. At the end of the loan, Brahim Diaz returned from Milan, as well as Fran Garcia from Rayo Vallecano. Midfielder Arda Guler was bought from Turkish Fenerbahce.
